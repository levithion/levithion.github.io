Neural Collaborative Filtering Movie Recommender

May 2025 - Jul 2025

Introduction

Hey there! Let me walk you through one of my favorite projects: a Neural Collaborative Filtering (NCF) Movie Recommender. Recommendations silently rule our digital lives, from the songs we stream to the products we buy. Personally speaking, I wanted to understand the magic behind these systems and build one myself from scratch. So, I set out to create a robust, deep learning-based engine that recommends movies you're actually going to love.

The Problem I Wanted to Solve

If you look at traditional recommendation systems, many rely on a technique called Matrix Factorization. It basically maps users and items to a shared latent space. While it's mathematically elegant, it has a glaring limitation: it assumes all relationships are strictly linear. But human taste isn't linear! Why someone likes both "The Matrix" and "Pride and Prejudice" might involve complex, subtle, and non-linear patterns. I needed an architecture that could capture these deep, hidden intricacies that standard algorithms simply miss.

How It Works (Under the Hood)

To solve this, I designed a hybrid model known as **NeuMF** (Neural Matrix Factorization), blending two powerful approaches:

  • Generalized Matrix Factorization (GMF): This component handles the linear interactions, serving as a strong, reliable baseline.
  • Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP): This deep neural network structure takes user and item embeddings and learns the complex, non-linear interactions between them.

By fusing these together in PyTorch, the model achieved a far more nuanced understanding of user preferences. But I didn't want this to just sit in a Jupyter notebook. I engineered a FastAPI backend to serve live predictions and wrapped it in an interactive Streamlit and Plotly dashboard. This meant users could interact with the model in real time, tweaking inputs and watching the recommendations adapt instantly.

Challenges & Learnings

The hardest part of this project was balancing the GMF and MLP branches during training. They converge at different rates, and fine-tuning the hyperparameters (like embedding sizes and learning rates) required a lot of methodical experimentation. Another significant challenge was ensuring the inference latency remained low when deployed, so the real-time Streamlit dashboard felt snappy and responsive.

Final Thoughts

This project was a massive leap for me. It bridged the gap between raw AI research and a living, breathing software product. Seeing the model successfully suggest a movie that perfectly matched a complex user profile was incredibly rewarding, and it proved to me the power of deep learning in personalization.
